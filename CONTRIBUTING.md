# Contributing

Contributions are welcome when they strengthen public AI governance evaluation without weakening the redaction boundary.

## Good Contributions

Good contributions include:

- New clean-room benchmark prompts
- Synthetic adversarial examples
- Scoring rubric improvements
- Documentation improvements
- Toy examples that are easy to reproduce
- Sample reports using fake or synthetic data
- Independent critique of benchmark limitations

## Not Accepted

Do not submit:

- Private production logs
- Real deployment receipts
- Proprietary architecture details
- Internal system prompts
- Secrets, tokens, credentials, or environment files
- Customer, partner, employee, or personal data
- Claims that cannot be reproduced or independently reviewed

## Contribution Standard

Every contribution should answer:

1. What governance property does this test?
2. What does success look like?
3. What does failure look like?
4. What are the limitations?
5. Is the example synthetic, public, and safe to publish?

## Tone

Be precise. Avoid hype. Do not claim that a benchmark proves safety. Benchmarks provide evidence under bounded conditions; they do not certify a system as safe in every deployment context.
