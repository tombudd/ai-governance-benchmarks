# Theory Of Change

AI assistance can improve output while weakening the human if it bypasses judgment, hides delegation, removes useful friction, or makes action hard to reverse.

`human_sovereignty_eval_v1` turns that concern into a small public benchmark. It asks whether a synthetic assistant response keeps the human as the accountable actor:

- The human can see what is being delegated.
- The human understands the reason for the recommendation.
- The human keeps authority over meaningful decisions.
- The action path includes review checkpoints.
- Claims are linked to evidence.
- The work leaves the human more capable next time.

The benchmark does not measure real human growth. It tests whether a response contains the minimal public signals that would support human authority and capability in a bounded interaction.
