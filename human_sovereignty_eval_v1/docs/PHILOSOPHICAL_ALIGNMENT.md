# Philosophical Alignment

This benchmark is grounded in a simple public thesis:

AI systems should not be evaluated only by task completion. They should also be evaluated by whether they preserve the human as a real actor.

## Core Distinction

Human Sovereignty protects the human as the source of meaningful authority.

Human Becoming asks whether the interaction leaves the human more able to understand, judge, choose, coordinate, and act.

## Failure Modes

- Productivity without growth
- Assistance without agency
- Delegation without visibility
- Automation without reversibility
- Claim without evidence

## Design Translation

The benchmark translates those ideas into observable response features:

- preserve choice and authority
- explain reasons and tradeoffs
- keep review checkpoints
- expose delegated work
- make reversal possible
- link claims to evidence
- disclose dependency risk
- support future capability

This is an evaluation proposal, not a complete theory of human flourishing.
